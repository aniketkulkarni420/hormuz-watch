Representation patterns — before / after

Same data we already collect. Same metrics that work. Different presentation. Each pair below shows a current "just-a-number-or-word" card on the left, and the proposed visual representation on the right. Open each pair, ask which one tells you the situation faster.

Principle: The human visual system parses position, size, length, hue, and slope in <200ms (pre-attentive). Reading a number with context — "Brent $107 is 49% above baseline" — requires 4 cognitive operations and 1.5+ seconds. Numbers and words alone are post-attentive. A dashboard of 30 metrics presented as text is asking the user to do 45 seconds of cognitive work before forming a judgement. Visual representation cuts that to under 5 seconds.

2. Verdict — band + score → arc gauge

The 4-band scale (NORMAL/ELEVATED/HIGH/CRITICAL) IS a gauge. Treat it like one.
Before · banded text + decimal
ELEVATED
1.7 / 4.0
Structural ELEVATED · no overrides fired.
The decimal is anchoring noise. "1.7" doesn't map to a felt sense. The word "ELEVATED" requires recalling the 4-band ladder.
After · arc gauge with zones
ELEVATED
1.7 / 4.0 · needle past mid-elevated
Structural ELEVATED · no overrides fired. Two notches from CRITICAL.
What the eye does: sees needle at second of four zones, immediately knows "in the second band, closer to HIGH than to NORMAL." No decimal-decoding, no band-name memorisation.

3. IRR spread — zone-banded scale

Bands (<15 aligned / 15–40 stressed / 40–80 dislocated / 80+ extreme) ARE the meaning. Show position in band, not just the label.
Before · number + label
Currency IRR spread 47% (dislocated)
User reads "47%" then "(dislocated)" — two values to integrate. Where in dislocated? Just above the threshold (40) or near extreme (80)? Word doesn't tell you.

Real implementation: Leaflet/Mapbox with proper coastline projection. Visual variable encoding shown here as a legend pattern.
Before · uniform circle dots
