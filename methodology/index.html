Methodology

How every metric is computed · last reviewed 2026-05

Philosophy

Accuracy first, recency second, inference third. Every metric on the dashboard either comes from a primary data source we can name, or is explicitly labeled as an estimate. Where a source has a known lag (e.g. EIA published-T+2), the dashboard says so.

We avoid trade calls. The dashboard is an intelligence aggregator not a recommendation engine — even when verdicts read "Long FRO," they reference the historical playbook, not present advice.

Honest source ranking: when a feed has multiple tiers (e.g. live commodity → ETF proxy → daily EIA), the dashboard's source badge tells you which tier is currently active. If you see EIA DAILY instead of LIVE FEED, the price is up to 2 trading days stale by design.

Metric definitions

Transits / 24h

A gate-crossing count, not a snapshot. We define a virtual line at longitude 56.45°E (the strait's narrowest point) between latitude 26.20°N and 26.70°N. Every time a unique vessel's reported longitude crosses this line in either direction within the valid latitude band, it counts as one transit. Counter rolls forward 24 hours.

Why this matters: pre-existing dashboards typically count "vessels in a bounding box" — which includes anchored vessels at Fujairah or Bandar Abbas as "transits." Those vessels aren't transiting anything. Our metric only counts actual east↔west movement across the chokepoint.

Pre-crisis baseline: 42 transits/day (EIA-derived from 21M bbl/day average × ~500K bbl/vessel). The vessel traffic trend chart compares today's 24h count to this baseline.

Oil throughput estimate ("Oil transited today")

Computed each second:

throughput = (transits_24h / 42) × 21,000,000 bbl/day × Brent_price

When today's 24h transit count is 21 (half of baseline) and Brent is $109, the projected daily oil flow value is approximately $1.15 billion. The header ticker accumulates the elapsed-day fraction of this number in real time.

Dark traffic %

Computed as GFW_events / (AIS_in_corridor + GFW_events). GFW SAR data captures vessels that appear in satellite imagery but lack corresponding AIS positions (i.e., AIS turned off).

Thresholds: <8% normal · 8-15% elevated · 15-20% high · >20% critical (based on Iran sanctions-enforcement spikes 2024).

BDTI

Baltic Dirty Tanker Index — composite index of dirty-tanker spot freight rates published by Baltic Exchange. Currently updated weekly via Monday cron job. Direction/magnitude is the analyst signal; precise level less important.

Thresholds (level): 800-1100 normal · 1100-1500 tight · 1500-2000 stressed · >2000 crisis.

Thresholds (WoW %): <+5% normal · +5-15% rising · +15-25% high · >+25% critical.

India % via Hormuz

Static 62%. Sourced from PPAC (Petroleum Planning & Analysis Cell, Ministry of Petroleum & Natural Gas, Government of India) and EIA crude trade flow data, FY24-25. Reviewed quarterly.

Verdict thresholds

Every conclusion ("NORMAL", "ELEVATED", "HIGH", "CRITICAL") uses thresholds backed by published research:

  • Traffic vs baseline: <-12% slowdown · ±12% normal · -20% high · -30% closure scenario activating. Reference: UNCTAD chokepoint volatility studies; 2019 IRGC seizures saw -12% over 2 weeks; 2025 Israel-Iran exchanges saw -18%.
  • Dark traffic: see Dark Traffic % section above. References: GFW published research on shadow fleet operations; Atlantic Council 2024 Iran enforcement analysis.
  • BDTI: see BDTI section. Reference: Baltic Exchange 10-year historical range.
  • Brent-WTI spread: <$4 loose · $4-7 normal · $7-10 tight · >$10 stressed. Reference: EIA historical Atlantic-basin analysis.
  • Tension gauge: Low / Moderate / Elevated / High / Critical — 5-state composite driven by traffic + dark % + BDTI WoW.

What we don't claim

  • We don't claim our verdicts predict equity moves with statistical certainty. They reflect industry-standard interpretation frameworks.
  • We don't claim live ACLED data — that tile is hidden until the API integration is complete.
  • We don't claim every vessel in the bounding box is transiting Hormuz — the 24h gate count and the snapshot categories are separately labeled.
  • We don't make trade calls. We are not financial advisers. SEBI RA registration is for research dissemination, not portfolio recommendations.
