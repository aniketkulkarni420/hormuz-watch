Philosophy

Accuracy first, recency second, inference third. Every metric on the dashboard either comes from a verified live data feed, or is explicitly labelled as a reference/baseline. Where a feed has a known lag, the dashboard says so.

We avoid trade calls. The dashboard is an intelligence aggregator, not a recommendation engine. Verdict labels like "Long FRO" reference the historical playbook in a closure scenario, not present advice.

Honest source ranking: when a feed has multiple tiers (e.g. live commodity → daily reference), the dashboard's source badge tells you which tier is active. If you see EIA DAILY instead of LIVE FEED, the price is up to 2 trading days stale by design — not pretending to be live.

Metric definitions

Transits / 24h

A gate-crossing count, not a snapshot. We define a virtual line at the strait's narrowest point. Every time a unique vessel crosses this line in either direction within the valid latitude band, it counts as one transit. Counter rolls forward 24 hours.

Why this matters: pre-existing dashboards typically count "vessels in a bounding box" — which includes anchored vessels at nearby ports as "transits." Those vessels aren't transiting anything. Our metric only counts actual east↔west movement across the chokepoint.

Pre-crisis baseline: 42 transits/day (derived from published 21M bbl/day throughput at ~500K bbl/vessel average). The vessel traffic trend chart compares today's 24h count to this baseline.

Oil throughput estimate ("Oil transited today")

Computed continuously:

throughput = (transits_24h / 42_baseline) × 21M bbl/day × Brent_price

When today's 24h transit count is 21 (half of baseline) and Brent is $109, projected daily oil flow value is approximately $1.15 billion. The header ticker accumulates the elapsed-day fraction of this number in real time.

Brent and WTI prices

The dashboard reads from a tiered source chain. The top working tier is always used; failures degrade silently to the next:

  1. Live commodity feed — refreshed continuously during market hours
  2. Live ETF proxy — applied to a daily reference level when primary degrades
  3. Daily reference — accurate, 1-2 trading day lag

The pulse tag at the top of the Market Pulse panel always names the current active tier.

Dark traffic %

Computed as satellite_events / (live_AIS_in_corridor + satellite_events). Satellite SAR captures vessels that appear in imagery but lack corresponding AIS broadcasts.

Banded into normal / elevated / high / critical; specific cut-points calibrated against historical enforcement spikes and held in the private threshold config.

Freight index (BDTI)

Composite index of dirty-tanker spot freight rates. Currently refreshed weekly. Direction and magnitude carry the analyst signal more than precise level.

Level + week-on-week change are banded into normal / tight / stressed / crisis. Specific band cut-points held in the private threshold config and recalibrated against the 10-year freight history each quarter.

India % via Hormuz

Static 62%. Sourced from official Indian government statistics and crude trade flow analysis, FY24-25. Reviewed quarterly.

Verdict thresholds

Every conclusion ("NORMAL", "ELEVATED", "HIGH", "CRITICAL") uses thresholds informed by published academic research, operational baselines from historical chokepoint events, and standard maritime analytical frameworks. Calibration constants live in config/verdict_thresholds.json and are recalibrated quarterly from the 2019–2024 disruption-episode window. The "crisis-average transit days" anchor (18 d) is one such constant, drawn from the same window and refreshed at each recalibration cycle.

  • Traffic vs baseline: tightened versus general chokepoint volatility (Hormuz is steadier than open shipping); calibrated to documented crisis-period drops.
  • Dark traffic %: aligned with shadow-fleet research and enforcement event analysis.
  • BDTI level + WoW: derived from 10-year historical range and observed catalyst-vs-fear-premium patterns.
  • Brent-WTI spread: reflects Atlantic-basin tightness and Hormuz-risk premium pricing.
  • Tension gauge: 5-state composite driven by traffic + dark % + BDTI WoW.

Composite signal methodology

When live vessel AIS feeds are degraded or unavailable, the verdict engine switches to a composite-signal mode. The weighting and source list:

SignalSourceAIS-primary weightComposite-fallback weight

Brent price + 24h spikeEIA / OilPriceAPI / Yahoo / FRED20%25%
Tanker stocks (6-stock index)Yahoo / Stooq15%20%

Earthquakes (Iran/Gulf, 7d)USGS FDSN5%5%
Weather / sea stateOpenWeather (4 Hormuz pts)5%5%
BDTI level + WoWBaltic Exchange manual5%10%

Each signal is scored 0 (calm) to 4 (critical). The weighted score maps to a verdict band: > 3 CRITICAL, > 2 HIGH, > 1 ELEVATED, otherwise NORMAL. Missing signals do not bias the score downward — the active weights are re-normalised across whichever sources are alive.

Why this exists: the primary AIS feed has been degraded for multiple weeks. Rather than show zeros, the dashboard surfaces an honest composite estimate while clearly labelling the mode in the banner and source chips.

Limitations of composite mode: it cannot detect a vessel-specific incident (e.g. seizure of a single tanker) that does not move oil price, tanker equities, news tone, aircraft activity, or weather. It will catch sustained disruption, not point events.

What we don't claim

  • We don't claim our verdicts predict equity moves with statistical certainty. They reflect industry-standard interpretation frameworks.
  • We don't claim live conflict-event data — that tile is hidden until the integration is complete.
  • We don't claim every vessel in the bounding box is transiting Hormuz — the 24h gate count and the snapshot categories are separately labeled.
  • We don't make trade calls. This is intelligence + interpretation, not financial advice.
