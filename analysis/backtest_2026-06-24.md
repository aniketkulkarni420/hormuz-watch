# Verdict Backtest — 2026-06-24 (#1)

**Question:** does the composite verdict actually anticipate Hormuz-driven market
moves, and does it beat the naive baseline "just watch the oil premium"?

**Harness:** `scripts/backtest.mjs` (pure Node, reusable) → `/api/backtest` (D1
history). Re-run anytime: `node scripts/backtest.mjs`.

**Data:** 603 raw snapshots → **44 daily observations** (2026-05-11 → 06-24).
Predictor (today) vs FORWARD % move at 1/3/7 days. Spearman rank correlation.

---

## ⚠️ Read this first — the data can't carry strong conclusions

- **n ≈ 44 days, dominated by ONE event** (the Iran war arc: escalation → thaw).
  Nothing here is statistically significant. This is a *directional sanity
  check* and a *reusable harness*, not a validation.
- **Stored verdicts are a MIX of engine versions** (the engine changed under us
  over these weeks). They had all 13 signals live at the time, though.
- **10 of 13 signals were never persisted** — only band/score/oil/bdti/transits
  are in D1 history. So only oil, freight, transits, and the composite itself
  can be tested at all. (Fixed going forward — see end.)

## Findings

### 1. The verdict does NOT predict oil. It mean-reverts.
Spearman vs forward **Brent** return: stored verdict **−0.30 / −0.23 / −0.29**
(1/3/7d). Negative = when the verdict was high, oil tended to **fall** next.
That's mean-reversion, not foresight — the verdict is oil-driven, so it inherits
oil's tendency to revert. **A HIGH verdict has historically been followed by oil
dropping.** Watching the verdict to forecast oil is pointless.

### 2. It does NOT beat naive oil-watching for oil.
At the 3-day Brent horizon, stored verdict (−0.23) vs naive oil-premium (−0.15)
— the composite is no better (slightly worse) than just looking at the oil
premium. The 13-signal machinery adds nothing over oil *for predicting oil.*

### 3. The ONE positive sign: freight (BDTI), the Hormuz-specific outcome.
BDTI (tanker freight) is more Hormuz-specific than Brent (which is global macro).
Here the **full composite behaves differently and correctly**:

| stored band | n | mean next-3d Brent | mean next-3d BDTI |
|---|---|---|---|
| NORMAL | 11 | −3.3% | **−3.4%** |
| ELEVATED | 16 | −0.6% | **−3.9%** |
| HIGH | 4 | −5.6% | **+1.7%** |
| CRITICAL | 11 | −3.5% | **+2.6%** |

The BDTI column is monotonic-ish in the right direction: **high bands precede
freight RISES, low bands precede freight FALLS.** And the stored composite
(+0.12 on BDTI-3d) beat naive oil (−0.50, pure mean-reversion). So the *non-oil
signals* are where any forward value lives — faint, but pointing the right way.

## What this means

- **Reposition expectations: the verdict is a COINCIDENT risk-state read, not a
  forecaster.** It describes the temperature now; it does not predict oil. Sell
  it as "what's the Hormuz risk state right now + why," never "what oil will do."
- **Oil is a good *descriptor*, a bad *predictor*** (mean-reverts). Keep it for
  the current read; stop implying it forecasts.
- **Freight (BDTI) is the most Hormuz-specific outcome** and where the composite
  showed (weak) skill → weight it well; it's the best validation target.
- **The tool is too young to be validated.** 44 days, one event. Real validation
  needs months of data across multiple events — and the signal vector persisted.

## Highest-value follow-up (done in this commit)

D1 now persists the **per-signal levels + confidence + regime** in the verdict
column (was band/score only). This starts the historical record so EACH signal
can be validated as data accumulates — the precondition for an evidence-based #2
(which signals to cut). It can't recover the past; it builds the future.

**Re-run cadence:** monthly, or after any notable Hormuz event. As n grows and
spans multiple events, the correlations gain meaning.
